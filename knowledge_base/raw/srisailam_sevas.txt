Q: What are the daily sevas at Srisailam temple?
A: The daily sevas at Srisailam include Suprabhata Seva (4:30 AM - waking the deity with Vedic chants), Rudrabhishekam (morning and evening - sacred abhishekam removing negative karma), Panchamruta Abhishekam (ritual bath with milk, curd, ghee, honey and sugar), Ashtottara Archana (chanting 108 names of Lord Shiva), Kumkum Archana for Goddess Bhramaramba, and Harathi Sevas (morning, afternoon and evening).

Q: What are the paid sevas (Arjitha Sevas) at Srisailam?
A: Arjitha Sevas at Srisailam include Sparsha Darshan (touching the Shiva Lingam - limited slots), Abhishekam Seva (Rudrabhishekam and Panchamruta), Kalyanotsavam (divine marriage ceremony for marital harmony), Pallaki Seva (evening deity procession), Sahasranamarchana (chanting 1000 names) and Ekadasa Rudrabhishekam (advanced 11 recitation ritual).

Q: What are the special sevas at Srisailam during festivals?
A: Special festival sevas include Maha Rudrabhishekam during Maha Shivaratri, Laksha Bilwarchana (offering 100,000 bilva leaves) during Karthika Masam, Chandi Homam during Navratri for protection and prosperity, and Shani Trayodashi Seva on Shani Trayodashi days.

Q: What is Suprabhata Seva at Srisailam?
A: Suprabhata Seva is performed at approximately 4:30 AM — the first seva of the day where the deity is woken up with Vedic chants and prayers. This is the most auspicious time to seek Lord Mallikarjuna's blessings as the Lord is believed to be in a gracious and peaceful mood at dawn.

Q: What is Kalyanotsavam at Srisailam?
A: Kalyanotsavam is the divine marriage ceremony of Lord Mallikarjuna and Goddess Bhramaramba. It is one of the most auspicious sevas at Srisailam. Devotees who attend or sponsor Kalyanotsavam are blessed with harmony in married life and family prosperity. Advance booking is strongly recommended.

Q: How to book sevas at Srisailam?
A: Sevas at Srisailam can be booked through the official temple website srisailadevasthanam.org or at temple counters in Srisailam. Advance booking is recommended especially for Kalyanotsavam, Sparsha Darshan and all festival sevas. Carry valid ID proof and report at least 30-60 minutes before seva time.

Q: What are the guidelines for attending sevas at Srisailam?
A: For sevas at Srisailam devotees must wear traditional attire, carry valid ID proof for booked sevas, and report at least 30-60 minutes before seva time. Mobile phones may be restricted inside the temple premises. Seva timings may change during festivals. Tickets are subject to availability.
