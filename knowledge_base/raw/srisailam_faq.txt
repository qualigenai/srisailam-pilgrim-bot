--- CATEGORY: TIMINGS ---

Q: What are the temple opening and closing times?
A: Srisailam temple is open daily. Morning darshan starts at 6:30 AM and closes at 1:00 PM. Evening darshan opens at 3:00 PM and closes at 10:00 PM. The temple remains closed between 1:00 PM and 3:00 PM for noon rituals.

Q: What time does the first pooja (Maha Neerajanam) happen?
A: Maha Neerajanam (morning abhishekam) is performed at 6:30 AM. Devotees should arrive by 6:00 AM to be present for it.

Q: What time is Abhishekam performed every day?
A: Rudrabhishekam is performed from 6:30 AM to 7:30 AM. Special abhishekam tickets can be booked in advance through the temple trust website or Mana Mitra portal.

Q: What is the last entry time for darshan?
A: Last entry for morning darshan is 12:30 PM. Last entry for evening darshan is 9:30 PM.

Q: Is the temple open on all days including weekdays?
A: Yes, the temple is open every day of the year including Mondays, public holidays, and festivals. During major festivals, timing may be extended.

Q: What time is Pancha Harathi performed?
A: Pancha Harathi is performed at 10:00 PM after the evening darshan session, before the temple closes. It is a grand ritual and very auspicious to witness.

Q: Is there any special darshan time for senior citizens and differently-abled pilgrims?
A: Yes, there is a special queue for senior citizens (above 60 years), differently-abled, and pregnant women. They can enter via the special gate near the main entrance and generally get faster access to darshan.

Q: Are there any changes in timings during festivals?
A: Yes, during major festivals like Mahashivaratri, Brahmotsavam, and Kartika Masam, the temple opens earlier (around 4:00 AM) and closes later (around midnight or later). Always confirm current timings at the temple website.

Q: What time does the evening Deeparadhana happen?
A: Evening Deeparadhana is performed at 8:00 PM. It is a beautiful lamp-waving ceremony at the sanctum and highly recommended to witness.

Q: Can I do darshan early in the morning before 6:30 AM?
A: The sanctum is not open before 6:30 AM. However, you can enter the temple premises and witness the preparation rituals and temple ambiance from around 6:00 AM.

Q: What is the most important festival at Srisailam?

Q: What is Brahmotsavam at Srisailam?
A: Brahmotsavam is a 9-day grand festival dedicated to Lord Mallikarjuna and Goddess Bhramaramba. It is celebrated during the Telugu month of Bhadrapadamu (August–September). The deity is taken out in various vahanas (vehicles) each day.

Q: What happens during Kartika Masam at Srisailam?

Q: Is there a special significance of visiting Srisailam on Mondays?
A: Monday (Somavar) is considered especially auspicious for Lord Shiva. The temple is usually crowded on Mondays. Special Somavarabhishekam is performed and many pilgrims observe fasting (Somavara Vratam) on this day.

Q: What is Ugadi significance at Srisailam?
A: Ugadi (Telugu New Year, usually March–April) marks new beginnings. Special rituals and poojas are conducted and the new year Panchangam (almanac) is read out at the temple. It is considered very auspicious to visit Srisailam on Ugadi.

Q: What is the significance of Karthika Pournami at Srisailam?
A: Karthika Pournami (full moon in the month of Kartika) is a major festival. Devotees light lamps near the Krishna River (Patala Ganga) and take a holy dip. The temple performs special annadanam and rituals through the night.

Q: Are there any special poojas on Amavasya (new moon) days?
A: Yes, Amavasya is significant at Srisailam. Devotees perform Pitru Tarpanams (ancestor prayers) at Patala Ganga on Amavasya. The temple also conducts special rituals on this day.

Q: When is the Sivaratri celebrated with an all-night vigil?
A: Apart from Mahashivaratri (once a year), Pradosham (occurs twice a month on the 13th day of each lunar fortnight) is observed with evening Pradosha Puja. Monthly Shivaratri (Krishna Chaturdashi) also has special observances.

Q: What is the festival calendar for the year?
A: Major festivals at Srisailam include: Mahashivaratri (Feb/Mar), Ugadi (Mar/Apr), Brahmotsavam (Aug/Sep), Navaratri (Sep/Oct), Kartika Masam (Oct/Nov), Karthika Pournami (Nov), and Dhanurmasam (Dec/Jan). Exact dates vary each year per the Hindu lunar calendar.

--- CATEGORY: HISTORY & SIGNIFICANCE ---

Q: Why is Srisailam called a Jyotirlinga?
A: Srisailam is home to Mallikarjuna Swamy, one of the 12 Jyotirlingas — the most sacred Shiva temples in India. The Jyotirlinga is believed to be a column of light (jyoti) that is a direct manifestation of Lord Shiva himself, not installed by humans but self-manifested.

Q: What is the significance of Bhramaramba Devi at Srisailam?
A: Bhramaramba Devi is the presiding goddess at Srisailam and is one of the 18 Maha Shakti Peethas in India. It is extremely rare and auspicious for a temple to be both a Jyotirlinga and a Shakti Peetha. Srisailam is one of such rare temples.

Q: What is the story behind the name "Srisailam"?
A: "Srisailam" means "sacred mountain" — "Sri" meaning sacred/auspicious and "Sailam" meaning hill/mountain. The temple is situated on the Nallamala hills on the banks of the Krishna River, giving it a majestic setting.

Q: What is Patala Ganga at Srisailam?
A: Patala Ganga is the name given to the Krishna River at Srisailam. "Patala" means underground/deep, referring to how the river flows deep in the valley below the temple. Taking a holy dip in Patala Ganga before darshan is considered very auspicious.

Q: How old is Srisailam temple?
A: Srisailam temple is believed to be over 2,000 years old. References to Srisailam are found in ancient texts like the Mahabharata, Skanda Purana, and Sivarahasya. The current structure has been renovated and expanded over centuries by Chalukya, Kakatiya, Vijayanagara, and other dynasties.

Q: Which kings built or renovated Srisailam temple?
A: The temple was built and renovated by multiple dynasties: Satavahanas, Ikshvakus, Chalukyas, Kakatiyas, Vijayanagara kings (especially Krishnadevaraya who made major contributions), and later by the Qutb Shahi and Asaf Jahi rulers.

Q: What does the Mallikarjuna name mean?
A: "Mallikarjuna" is a combined name — "Malli" means jasmine flower (after Goddess Parvati who loves jasmine), and "Arjuna" is another name of Lord Shiva meaning white/pure. Together it means "Shiva who is white like jasmine."

Q: Is Srisailam mentioned in the Mahabharata?
A: Yes. Srisailam is mentioned in the Aranyaparva chapter of the Mahabharata where it is listed as a sacred pilgrimage site. The Pandavas are said to have visited this sacred hill during their forest exile.

Q: What is the importance of the Akshamala at Srisailam?
A: The Akshamala is the sacred ornament (rosary) of Lord Shiva preserved at Srisailam. It is displayed during special rituals. Devotees who see the Akshamala are believed to be freed from the cycle of birth and death.

Q: What is the Shikhara (tower) style of Srisailam temple?
A: Srisailam temple features a Dravidian architectural style with a massive Rajagopuram (main tower). The gopuram is ornately carved with mythological figures and is visible from a great distance. The temple was significantly expanded during the Vijayanagara period.

A: Yes. Drinking water is available free at multiple points within and around the temple complex. The temple trust maintains water facilities for pilgrims.

Q: What should I not bring inside Srisailam temple?
A: Prohibited items: mobile phones (must be deposited in lockers), cameras, bags, footwear (removed at the shoe stand), leather items (belts, wallets, bags), alcohol, cigarettes, and non-vegetarian food.

Q: Should I remove footwear before entering?
A: Yes. Footwear must be removed before entering the temple premises. Shoe stands and paid cloak rooms are available near the entrance.

Q: Can I wear jeans to Srisailam temple?
A: Jeans are generally permitted but you should also wear a proper top — no torn jeans, sleeveless tops, or shorts. Traditional attire is strongly preferred. Dhoti for men and saree/salwar for women is recommended.

Q: Are there any specific rules for women visiting Srisailam?
A: Women are welcome at all times. There are no restrictions based on menstrual cycle for entering the outer temple premises at Srisailam. However, it is a personal choice per faith and tradition.

Q: Is silent behavior required inside the temple?
A: You should maintain reverence inside the temple. Loud talking, using phones, and running around are discouraged. Devotional singing and chanting of "Om Namah Shivaya" are always welcome.

Q: Can I offer flowers or bilva leaves to the deity?
A: Flowers, bilva (bel) leaves, and other traditional offerings can be purchased from vendors outside the temple. Hand these over to priests at the appropriate point inside. Bringing flowers into the queue is generally allowed.

--- CATEGORY: NEARBY ATTRACTIONS ---

Q: What are the places to visit near Srisailam?
A: Key attractions near Srisailam: (1) Patala Ganga — holy river ghat at the base of the hill. (2) Akkamahadevi Caves — ancient cave complex with Shiva shrines. (3) Sakshi Ganapathi temple — temple of the witnessing Ganesha, must-visit before main darshan. (4) Hatakeswaram — ancient Shiva temple. (5) Srisailam Dam — a major hydroelectric dam, beautiful picnic spot. (6) Nallamala Forest — scenic forest with wildlife including tigers.

Q: What is the Sakshi Ganapathi temple at Srisailam?
A: Sakshi Ganapathi temple is located near the main entrance of Srisailam temple. Devotees traditionally visit this temple first before the main Mallikarjuna darshan. It is believed that Ganesha here acts as a witness (sakshi) to your pilgrimage.

Q: How far is Srisailam Dam from the temple?
A: Srisailam Dam is approximately 3 km from the main temple. It is a spectacular dam on the Krishna River and offers beautiful views. The dam viewpoint is a popular photography spot.

Q: Can I visit Akkamahadevi Caves?
A: Akkamahadevi Caves are accessible by trekking from Srisailam. The caves have ancient Shiva shrines and are named after the Kannada saint-poetess Akkamahadevi. Trekking permits may be required from the forest department. It is best visited during winter months.

Q: Is Srisailam inside a wildlife sanctuary?
A: Yes, Srisailam is located within the Nagarjunasagar-Srisailam Tiger Reserve, one of the largest tiger reserves in India. This makes the surrounding forests rich in biodiversity. The forest area is protected, so visitors should not litter or disturb wildlife.

Q: What is the Bhramaramba temple separate from the main Mallikarjuna temple?
A: Bhramaramba Devi has her own shrine inside the Srisailam temple complex. This shrine is a Maha Shakti Peetha. Devotees who come to Srisailam make it a point to have darshan of both Mallikarjuna (Shiva) and Bhramaramba (Shakti).

A: Yes, there are ATMs in Srisailam town near the temple. Major banks like SBI, Andhra Bank, and others have ATM facilities. However, during festivals, ATMs may run out of cash — carry sufficient cash in advance.

A: Mobile connectivity is available in Srisailam town and temple area (Jio, Airtel, and BSNL generally work). The deep forest stretches on the way to Srisailam may have weak or no signal.

Q: Is Srisailam suitable for family trips with children?
A: Absolutely. Srisailam is a great family pilgrimage destination. The dam, forest surroundings, and temple make it an enriching experience. The temple has facilities for children and families. Carry water, snacks, and comfortable clothing for kids.

Q: Can I visit Srisailam during monsoon season?
A: Visiting during peak monsoon (July–August) can be challenging as the ghat roads become slippery and the forest can have heavy rain. However, the surroundings are lush and beautiful. Check road conditions before visiting. The dam viewpoint is especially spectacular during monsoon.

Q: What is the best time of year to visit Srisailam?
A: The best time to visit Srisailam is between October and March (post-monsoon to winter). The weather is pleasant, the surroundings are green, and darshan queues are relatively manageable on non-festival days.

Q: Is Srisailam a part of the Pancha Bhuta Stalas or Dwadasha Jyotirlingas?
A: Srisailam is one of the Dwadasha Jyotirlingas (12 sacred Shiva temples). It is not part of the Pancha Bhuta Stalas (which are 5 temples representing the 5 elements). However, it is part of the Shakti Peetha network as well, making it doubly significant.

Q: What is the significance of walking barefoot in Srisailam?
A: Many devoted pilgrims walk barefoot from the town entrance to the temple and on the temple premises as an act of devotion and humility before Lord Shiva. The hot stone paths can be challenging, so pilgrims are advised to carry water and consider timing (early morning is cooler).

Q: What is Jai Mallikarjuna?
A: "Jai Mallikarjuna" is the traditional greeting and devotional chant of Srisailam devotees. It means "Victory to Lord Mallikarjuna." Saying "Jai Mallikarjuna" is a way of invoking the blessings of the presiding deity and is used as both a greeting and an expression of devotion.
