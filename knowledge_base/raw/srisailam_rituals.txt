Q: What is Suprabhata Seva at Srisailam?
A: Suprabhata Seva is the first ritual of the day at Srisailam performed at approximately 4:30 AM. The deity Lord Mallikarjuna is woken up with Vedic hymns and prayers. This is the most auspicious time to seek the Lord's blessings as he is believed to be in a very gracious mood at dawn.

Q: What is Rudrabhishekam at Srisailam?
A: Rudrabhishekam is one of the most important sevas at Srisailam performed both in morning and evening sessions. The Shivalinga is bathed with water, milk, curd, honey, ghee and other sacred substances while chanting Sri Rudram. It removes all doshas and sins and brings prosperity. Devotees can book online at srisailadevasthanam.org.

Q: What is Ekadasa Rudrabhishekam at Srisailam?
A: Ekadasa Rudrabhishekam is an advanced and highly powerful form of Rudrabhishekam where Sri Rudram is chanted 11 times during the abhishekam. It is considered extremely auspicious and is performed for major spiritual blessings, removal of serious obstacles and for moksha.

Q: What is Panchamruta Abhishekam at Srisailam?
A: Panchamruta Abhishekam involves bathing Lord Mallikarjuna with five sacred substances — milk, curd, ghee, honey and sugar. Each substance has specific spiritual significance. Milk represents purity, curd brings prosperity, ghee grants wisdom, honey brings sweetness to life and sugar grants happiness.

Q: What is the significance of Abhishekam at Srisailam?
A: Abhishekam at Srisailam is considered equivalent to bathing in all holy rivers simultaneously. Water, milk, honey, curd, ghee, sugarcane juice and sandal paste are used. Performing or witnessing Abhishekam of the Jyotirlinga at Srisailam is believed to grant liberation from the cycle of birth and death.

Q: What is Ashtottara Satanamarchana at Srisailam?
A: Ashtottara Satanamarchana involves chanting the 108 names of Lord Shiva with offerings of flowers and bilva leaves to Lord Mallikarjuna. It is an affordable and highly meritorious seva that any devotee can perform. Each name represents a divine quality of Lord Shiva.

Q: What is Bilva Archana at Srisailam?
A: Bilva Archana is offering bilva leaves to Lord Mallikarjuna while chanting his names. Bilva leaves are considered most sacred to Lord Shiva. Each leaf is said to remove sins equivalent to great austerities. This seva is very affordable and extremely auspicious at Srisailam.

Q: What is Laksha Bilwarchana at Srisailam?
A: Laksha Bilwarchana is the offering of one lakh (100,000) bilva leaves to Lord Mallikarjuna. It is performed especially during Karthika Masam. This is considered one of the most powerful rituals at Srisailam and is believed to grant all wishes and spiritual liberation.

Q: What is Kumkumarchana at Srisailam?
A: Kumkumarchana is a special pooja offered to Goddess Bhramarambika Devi using kumkum (vermillion) while chanting her 108 names. It is performed for health, wealth, prosperity and removal of obstacles. Highly recommended for women devotees seeking the Goddess's blessings.

Q: What is Sahasranama Archana at Srisailam?
A: Sahasranama Archana involves chanting the thousand names of Lord Mallikarjuna or Goddess Bhramarambika. It is one of the most powerful sevas at Srisailam performed by temple priests on behalf of devotees. The chanting takes approximately one hour and grants immense spiritual merit.

Q: What is Maha Nivedana and Harathi at Srisailam?
A: Maha Nivedana is the offering of food (prasadam) to Lord Mallikarjuna and Goddess Bhramarambika in the afternoon. This is followed by Harathi — waving of lamps before the deity. Witnessing the Harathi at Srisailam is considered very auspicious and purifying.

Q: What is Sandhya Harathi at Srisailam?
A: Sandhya Harathi is the evening lamp offering performed at dusk at Srisailam. It is one of the most beautiful and spiritually uplifting experiences at the temple. The entire sanctum is illuminated with lamps while devotional music and Vedic chanting fill the air.

Q: What is Ekanta Seva at Srisailam?
A: Ekanta Seva is the final seva of the day after the temple closes to the public. The deity is prepared for rest — bathed, dressed in night clothes and offered food. Attending Ekanta Seva is considered extremely auspicious as one witnesses the Lord in a most intimate divine moment. Limited tickets available and must be booked well in advance.

Q: What is Pallaki Seva at Srisailam?
A: Pallaki Seva is the evening procession of Lord Mallikarjuna in a decorated palanquin within the temple premises. Devotees gather to have a glimpse of the Lord during this procession. The procession is accompanied by music, chanting and floral offerings. It is a spiritually uplifting experience.

Q: What is Kalyanotsavam at Srisailam?
A: Kalyanotsavam is the celestial wedding ceremony of Lord Mallikarjuna and Goddess Bhramarambika performed at Srisailam on special occasions and full moon days. The deities are decorated as a divine couple and the wedding ritual is performed. Attending Kalyanotsavam is believed to bless devotees with marital happiness and family prosperity.

Q: What is Chandi Homam at Srisailam?
A: Chandi Homam is a powerful fire ritual performed at Srisailam especially during Navratri for Goddess Bhramarambika Devi. It invokes the divine protective power of the Goddess and is performed for divine protection, prosperity and removal of negative energies. The Homam is conducted by trained Vedic priests.

Q: What is Shani Trayodashi Abhishekam at Srisailam?
A: Shani Trayodashi Abhishekam is a special ritual performed on Shani Trayodashi days — when Saturday coincides with the 13th lunar day. This combination is considered especially powerful for Lord Shiva worship. Performing abhishekam on this day at Srisailam is believed to remove Shani dosham and bring divine blessings.

Q: What are the different types of darshan at Srisailam?
A: Srisailam offers four types of darshan — Sarva Darshan (free general darshan for all devotees), Seeghra Darshan (faster paid darshan with shorter waiting time), Athi Seeghra Darshan (quickest paid darshan), and Sparsha Darshan (rare privilege to touch the Jyotirlinga on specific days). Online booking available at srisailadevasthanam.org.

Q: What is Sparsha Darshan at Srisailam?
A: Sparsha Darshan is the most sacred and rare form of darshan at Srisailam where devotees get to physically touch the Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga. It is available on limited days with restricted numbers. Aadhaar card is mandatory. Tokens are distributed on the day of darshan. It is considered the highest blessing one can receive at Srisailam.

Q: What is the significance of Mondays at Srisailam?
A: Mondays are especially sacred at Srisailam as it is the holy day of Lord Shiva. Special pujas and abhishekams are performed every Monday. Devotee footfall is higher. It is recommended to book darshan tickets in advance for Monday visits to avoid long waiting times.

Q: What is Pradosha at Srisailam?
A: Pradosha is a sacred time period occurring twice monthly on the 13th lunar day. During Pradosha special Shiva pujas are performed at Srisailam. Lord Shiva is believed to be in a very blissful state during this time and grants wishes readily. Special sevas are available and advance booking is recommended.

Q: What should pilgrims carry for sevas at Srisailam?
A: For sevas at Srisailam carry your Aadhaar card, printed booking confirmation, and wear traditional attire. For Abhishekam sevas bring clean dhoti or saree. Flowers and offerings can be purchased at temple shops. Mobile phones are not permitted inside the sanctum sanctorum. Report to the seva counter at least 30 minutes before scheduled time.

Q: What is the spiritual significance of Srisailam temple?
A: Srisailam is considered Kailasa on Earth — the abode of Lord Shiva. It is one of the rarest sacred sites combining both a Jyotirlinga and a Shakti Peetha. Performing Rudrabhishekam and Bilwarchana here is believed to grant moksha. Ancient texts state that merits earned at Srisailam equal worshipping all divine powers of the universe.

Q: What are important notes for sevas at Srisailam?
A: Timings for sevas may vary on festival days. Advance booking is strongly recommended especially during Maha Shivaratri, Karthika Masam and Navratri. All sevas can be booked online at srisailadevasthanam.org. Strict dress code applies — traditional Indian attire mandatory. High rush is expected during major festivals so plan accordingly.
