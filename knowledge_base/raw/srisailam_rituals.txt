Srisailam Temple Rituals and Sevas
Temple: Sri Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple, Srisailam
Location: Andhra Pradesh, India

OVERVIEW
Srisailam temple is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in India. It is unique as it is both a Jyotirlinga (Lord Shiva as Mallikarjuna) and a Shakti Peetha (Goddess Bhramaramba). The temple offers various daily, weekly, and festival sevas (ritual services) for devotees.

----------------------------------------
DAILY SEVAS (NITYA KAINKARYAMS)
----------------------------------------

1. Suprabhata Seva
- Time: Early morning (~4:30 AM)
- Description: Waking up the deity with Vedic hymns and prayers.

2. Rudrabhishekam
- Time: Morning and evening sessions
- Description: Sacred abhishekam to Lord Mallikarjuna using water, milk, curd, honey, and other holy items.
- Significance: One of the most important Shiva rituals for removing doshas and seeking blessings.

3. Panchamruta Abhishekam
- Description: Abhishekam using five sacred substances:
  milk, curd, ghee, honey, and sugar.
- Purpose: Purification and divine blessings.

4. Ashtottara Satanamarchana
- Description: Chanting 108 names of Lord Shiva with offerings of flowers and bilva leaves.

5. Kumkum Archana (Bhramaramba Devi)
- Description: Special pooja for Goddess Bhramaramba using kumkum.
- Purpose: Blessings for prosperity and well-being.

6. Maha Nivedana & Harathi
- Time: Afternoon
- Description: Offering food (prasadam) to the deity followed by harathi.

7. Evening Sevas
- Includes:
  - Sandhya Harathi
  - Evening Rudrabhishekam
  - Night rituals

----------------------------------------
SPECIAL SEVAS (ARJITHA SEVAS)
----------------------------------------

1. Ekadasa Rudrabhishekam
- Description: Advanced form of Rudrabhishekam performed with 11 recitations of Rudram.

2. Pallaki Seva
- Time: Evening
- Description: Procession of the deity in a palanquin within temple premises.

3. Sparsha Darshan
- Description: Devotees are allowed to touch the Shiva Lingam (on specific days/timings).

----------------------------------------
FESTIVAL & OCCASIONAL SEVAS
----------------------------------------

1. Kalyanotsavam
- Description: Celestial wedding of Lord Mallikarjuna and Goddess Bhramaramba.
- Performed on: Special occasions / Full moon days.

2. Maha Rudrabhishekam
- Performed during: Maha Shivaratri
- Significance: Highly powerful ritual for spiritual upliftment.

3. Laksha Bilwarchana
- Description: Offering of one lakh (100,000) bilva leaves to Lord Shiva.
- Performed during: Karthika Masam.

4. Chandi Homam
- Performed during: Navratri
- Purpose: Divine protection and prosperity.

5. Shani Trayodashi Abhishekam
- Special ritual performed on Shani Trayodashi days.

----------------------------------------
GENERAL DARSHAN TYPES
----------------------------------------

1. Free Darshan (Sarva Darshan)
2. Seeghra Darshan
3. Athi Seeghra Darshan
4. Sparsha Darshan (limited availability)

----------------------------------------
IMPORTANT NOTES
----------------------------------------

- Timings may vary on festival days.
- Advance booking is recommended for all paid sevas.
- Sevas can be booked online via official Srisailam Devasthanam portal.
- Dress code and temple rules must be followed strictly.
- High rush expected during Maha Shivaratri, Karthika Masam, and Navratri.

----------------------------------------
SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE
----------------------------------------

- Srisailam is considered equivalent to Mount Kailasa on earth.
- Performing Rudrabhishekam and Bilwarchana here is believed to grant moksha.
- The temple is one of the rare sites combining Shiva and Shakti worship.
