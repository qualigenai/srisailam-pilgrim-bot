SRISAILAM TEMPLE — FESTIVAL CALENDAR & SPECIAL DAYS
================================================================
Note: All dates follow the Hindu lunar calendar. Solar dates change
every year. Below are approximate months; verify exact dates at
srisailadevasthanam.org each year.

--- MAJOR ANNUAL FESTIVALS ---

FESTIVAL: Mahashivaratri
MONTH: Magha / Phalguna (February–March)
DURATION: 1 day + all-night vigil
SIGNIFICANCE: The holiest night of the year for Lord Shiva. Temple stays open all night. Four special poojas performed every 3 hours: at 9 PM, midnight, 3 AM, 6 AM. Lakhs of devotees attend.
SPECIAL ARRANGEMENTS: 24-hour darshan, extra buses, crowd management, free annadanam extended.
TIP: Arrive a day before for comfortable darshan. Book accommodation well in advance.

FESTIVAL: Ugadi (Telugu New Year)
MONTH: Chaitra (March–April)
DURATION: 1 day
SIGNIFICANCE: New Year celebrations at the temple. Panchangasravanam (almanac reading) performed. Special poojas and abhishekam for the new year.
TIP: Moderate crowd. Good time to visit for a festive yet manageable experience.

FESTIVAL: Rama Navami
MONTH: Chaitra (March–April)
DURATION: 1 day
SIGNIFICANCE: Birthday of Lord Rama. While primarily a Vishnu festival, Srisailam holds special poojas. The surrounding region celebrates with processions.

FESTIVAL: Vaikunta Ekadashi
MONTH: Margashira / Pushya (December)
DURATION: 1 day
SIGNIFICANCE: Though primarily a Vaishnava festival, Srisailam devotees observe this day. The temple has light rituals. Many pilgrims combine with Dhanurmasam visits.

FESTIVAL: Brahmotsavam
MONTH: Bhadrapadamu (August–September)
DURATION: 9 days
SIGNIFICANCE: The grandest annual festival of Srisailam. Lord Mallikarjuna is taken out in a procession on different vahanas each day: Day 1 – Pedda Sesha Vahanam, Day 2 – Chinna Sesha Vahanam, Day 3 – Simha Vahanam, Day 4 – Pearls Mantapam, Day 5 – Kalpavriksha Vahanam, Day 6 – Garuda Vahanam, Day 7 – Hanumanta Vahanam, Day 8 – Gaja Vahanam, Day 9 – Surya Prabha Vahanam (Rathotsavam — chariot festival).
TIP: Rathotsavam on Day 9 is the most spectacular. Huge crowds expected.

FESTIVAL: Navaratri / Dussehra
MONTH: Ashwina (September–October)
DURATION: 10 days
SIGNIFICANCE: Celebrated with great devotion at Bhramaramba Devi shrine. Special Kumkumarchana and Chandika Homa performed. Vijayadasami (Day 10) is especially auspicious.

FESTIVAL: Karthika Masam
MONTH: Karthika (October–November)
DURATION: Entire month (30 days)
SIGNIFICANCE: The entire month of Karthika is sacred to Shiva. Every Monday is celebrated as Karthika Somavar. Devotees light diyas (lamps) at Patala Ganga throughout the month. Abhishekam bookings are highest during this period.
TIP: One of the best times to visit for a deeply spiritual experience. Book accommodation 2–3 months in advance.

FESTIVAL: Karthika Pournami (Full Moon)
MONTH: Karthika (November)
DURATION: 1 day
SIGNIFICANCE: The most sacred day within Karthika Masam. Devotees take a holy dip in Patala Ganga (Krishna River) at dawn. Temple conducts all-night programs. Annadanam extended.

FESTIVAL: Dhanurmasam
MONTH: Margashira/Pushya (December–January)
DURATION: 1 month
SIGNIFICANCE: Auspicious month for devotional activities. Early morning (pre-dawn) rituals performed. Devotees wake at 4–5 AM for pre-dawn poojas.

FESTIVAL: Muharram (Srisailam local significance)
MONTH: Islamic calendar (varies)
SIGNIFICANCE: The Srisailam region has a tradition of communal harmony. Local observances are respectfully noted.

--- WEEKLY & MONTHLY SPECIAL DAYS ---

EVERY MONDAY (Somavar):
Special Somavarabhishekam performed. Highly auspicious for Shiva devotees. Heavy crowd expected, especially during Karthika Masam.

PRADOSHAM (twice a month):
Occurs on the 13th day (Trayodashi) of each lunar fortnight — once in the waxing phase (Shukla Paksha) and once in the waning phase (Krishna Paksha). Special Pradosha Puja performed in the evening around sunset. Considered auspicious for liberation from sins.

AMAVASYA (New Moon):
Pilgrims perform Pitru Tarpanams at Patala Ganga. Temple conducts special rituals. Significant for ancestor worship.

POURNAMI (Full Moon):
Special Pournami Abhishekam. Crowds higher than normal days. The full moon over the Nallamala hills and Krishna River is a breathtaking sight.

SHIVA RATRI (monthly):
Krishna Chaturdashi (14th day of dark fortnight) every month is observed as a mini Shivaratri. Special night puja performed.
