SRISAILAM TEMPLE — FESTIVAL CALENDAR Q&A (RAG TEST SET)

Q: What is the holiest night of the year at Srisailam Temple?
A: Mahashivaratri is the holiest night of the year for Lord Shiva at Srisailam Temple. The temple stays open all night, and four special poojas are performed every 3 hours — at 9 PM, midnight, 3 AM, and 6 AM.

Q: In which months does Mahashivaratri fall?
A: Mahashivaratri falls in the months of Magha or Phalguna, which corresponds to February–March in the Gregorian calendar.

Q: How long does Mahashivaratri last at Srisailam?
A: Mahashivaratri lasts 1 day along with an all-night vigil.

Q: What special arrangements are made during Mahashivaratri at Srisailam?
A: During Mahashivaratri, the temple offers 24-hour darshan, runs extra buses, implements crowd management measures, and extends free annadanam.

Q: What is the best tip for visiting Srisailam during Mahashivaratri?
A: It is recommended to arrive a day before for comfortable darshan and to book accommodation well in advance, as lakhs of devotees attend.

Q: What is Ugadi and when is it celebrated?
A: Ugadi is the Telugu New Year. It is celebrated in the month of Chaitra (March–April). At Srisailam, special poojas and abhishekam are performed for the new year, and the Panchangasravanam (almanac reading) is conducted.

Q: Is Ugadi a good time for a manageable visit to Srisailam?
A: Yes. Ugadi sees moderate crowds, making it a good time to visit for a festive yet manageable experience.

Q: What festival is celebrated for 9 days in August–September at Srisailam?
A: Brahmotsavam is the 9-day grand annual festival celebrated in the month of Bhadrapadamu (August–September).

Q: What happens during Brahmotsavam at Srisailam?
A: During Brahmotsavam, Lord Mallikarjuna is taken out in procession on a different vahana (vehicle) each day over 9 days.

Q: What are the vahanas used on each day of Brahmotsavam?
A: Day 1 – Pedda Sesha Vahanam, Day 2 – Chinna Sesha Vahanam, Day 3 – Simha Vahanam, Day 4 – Pearls Mantapam, Day 5 – Kalpavriksha Vahanam, Day 6 – Garuda Vahanam, Day 7 – Hanumanta Vahanam, Day 8 – Gaja Vahanam, Day 9 – Surya Prabha Vahanam (Rathotsavam, the chariot festival).

Q: Which day of Brahmotsavam is the most spectacular?
A: Day 9, known as Rathotsavam (the chariot festival), is the most spectacular day. Huge crowds are expected on this day.

Q: What festival is observed for 10 days in September–October at Srisailam?
A: Navaratri / Dussehra is observed for 10 days in the month of Ashwina (September–October).

Q: Which shrine is the focus of Navaratri celebrations at Srisailam?
A: The Bhramaramba Devi shrine is the focus of Navaratri celebrations. Special Kumkumarchana and Chandika Homa are performed.

Q: What is the significance of Vijayadasami at Srisailam?
A: Vijayadasami, the 10th day of Navaratri / Dussehra, is considered especially auspicious at Srisailam.

Q: What is Karthika Masam and how long does it last?
A: Karthika Masam is a month-long sacred period (30 days) in the month of Karthika (October–November), dedicated to Lord Shiva. Every Monday during this period is celebrated as Karthika Somavar.

Q: What do devotees do at Patala Ganga during Karthika Masam?
A: Devotees light diyas (lamps) at Patala Ganga throughout the month of Karthika Masam.

Q: Why are abhishekam bookings highest during Karthika Masam?
A: Karthika Masam is one of the most sacred periods for Shiva devotees, making it the peak pilgrimage season. This high demand causes abhishekam bookings to be at their highest.

Q: How far in advance should accommodation be booked for Karthika Masam?
A: Accommodation should be booked 2–3 months in advance for Karthika Masam.

Q: What is Karthika Pournami and why is it significant?
A: Karthika Pournami is the full moon day within Karthika Masam, occurring in November. It is the most sacred day of the month. Devotees take a holy dip in Patala Ganga (Krishna River) at dawn, and the temple conducts all-night programs with extended Annadanam.

Q: What is Dhanurmasam and when does it occur?
A: Dhanurmasam is an auspicious month for devotional activities observed in Margashira/Pushya (December–January). Devotees wake at 4–5 AM to participate in pre-dawn (early morning) poojas.

Q: What is the significance of every Monday at Srisailam Temple?
A: Every Monday (Somavar), a special Somavarabhishekam is performed. It is highly auspicious for Shiva devotees. Heavy crowds are expected, particularly during Karthika Masam.

Q: What is Pradosham and when does it occur?
A: Pradosham occurs twice a month on the 13th day (Trayodashi) of each lunar fortnight — once in the waxing phase (Shukla Paksha) and once in the waning phase (Krishna Paksha). A special Pradosha Puja is performed at sunset and is considered auspicious for liberation from sins.

Q: What rituals are performed on Amavasya (New Moon) at Srisailam?
A: On Amavasya, pilgrims perform Pitru Tarpanams at Patala Ganga and the temple conducts special rituals. This day is significant for ancestor worship.

Q: What is special about Pournami (Full Moon) at Srisailam?
A: A special Pournami Abhishekam is performed on the full moon day. Crowds are higher than on normal days, and the full moon over the Nallamala hills and Krishna River is described as a breathtaking sight.

Q: What is the monthly Shiva Ratri observance at Srisailam?
A: Krishna Chaturdashi — the 14th day of the dark fortnight (waning phase) every month — is observed as a mini Shivaratri. A special night puja is performed on this day.

Q: What is Vaikunta Ekadashi and how is it observed at Srisailam?
A: Vaikunta Ekadashi falls in the month of Margashira/Pushya (December). Though primarily a Vaishnava festival, Srisailam devotees observe the day with light rituals. Many pilgrims combine this visit with Dhanurmasam activities.

Q: What is Rama Navami and is it celebrated at Srisailam?
A: Rama Navami is the birthday of Lord Rama, celebrated in the month of Chaitra (March–April). While it is primarily a Vishnu festival, Srisailam holds special poojas, and the surrounding region celebrates with processions.

Q: Does the Srisailam region observe any Islamic festivals?
A: Yes. The Srisailam region has a tradition of communal harmony and locally observes Muharram respectfully. The exact date varies as it follows the Islamic calendar.

Q: What calendar system does the Srisailam festival calendar follow?
A: The Srisailam festival calendar follows the Hindu lunar calendar. Solar calendar dates change every year.

Q: Where can devotees verify the exact dates of Srisailam festivals?
A: Exact dates should be verified at the official website: srisailadevasthanam.org, or via the Panchangam (Hindu almanac).

Q: What is Patala Ganga?
A: Patala Ganga refers to the Krishna River at Srisailam. Devotees light lamps there during Karthika Masam, take holy dips on Karthika Pournami, and perform Pitru Tarpanams on Amavasya.

Q: What is Panchangasravanam?
A: Panchangasravanam is the reading of the Hindu almanac (Panchangam), performed at the temple during Ugadi (Telugu New Year) as part of the new year celebrations.

Q: Which festival at Srisailam is considered the grandest annual festival?
A: Brahmotsavam, the 9-day festival held in Bhadrapadamu (August–September), is considered the grandest annual festival of Srisailam.

Q: What is Kumkumarchana and during which festival is it performed at Srisailam?
A: Kumkumarchana is a ritual offering of kumkum (vermilion) to the deity. It is performed during Navaratri at the Bhramaramba Devi shrine.

Q: What is Chandika Homa and when is it performed at Srisailam?
A: Chandika Homa is a fire ritual dedicated to Goddess Chandika. It is performed during the Navaratri / Dussehra festival at Srisailam.

Q: How many special poojas are performed during the Mahashivaratri all-night vigil, and at what times?
A: Four special poojas are performed during the Mahashivaratri all-night vigil — at 9 PM, midnight, 3 AM, and 6 AM (approximately every 3 hours).

--- CORE IDENTITY ---

Q: What is Kumbhotsavam at Srisailam?
A: Kumbhotsavam is the annual festival celebrated at Srisailam in honour of Goddess Sri Bhramaramba Devi. It is a major ritualistic occasion dedicated exclusively to the presiding goddess, during which a large-scale Satvika Bali (a non-violent vegetarian offering) is made to her. The festival is observed with traditional pomp and special poojas performed in solitude (Ekantam).

Q: Is Kumbhotsavam dedicated to Lord Mallikarjuna or Goddess Bhramaramba?
A: Kumbhotsavam is dedicated exclusively to Goddess Sri Bhramaramba Devi — the presiding goddess of Srisailam. It is not a Mallikarjuna Swamy festival. In fact, arjitha sevas and darshan at the Lord Mallikarjuna temple are also suspended in the evening on the day of Kumbhotsavam, in deference to the goddess's festival.

--- WHEN IT HAPPENS ---

Q: In which month is Kumbhotsavam celebrated at Srisailam?
A: Kumbhotsavam is celebrated in the Telugu month of Chaitra, which corresponds to March–April in the Gregorian calendar.

Q: What is the exact rule for determining the date of Kumbhotsavam?
A: Kumbhotsavam is observed on the first Tuesday or Friday — whichever comes first — after the Pournami (Full Moon day) of Chaitra Masam. Because the Hindu lunar calendar shifts each year, the Gregorian date changes annually.

Q: What was the date of Kumbhotsavam in 2026?
A: In 2026, Kumbhotsavam was scheduled for April 7 (Tuesday). A preparatory meeting to review all arrangements was held on April 2, 2026, chaired by Temple Executive Officer M Srinivasa Rao.

Q: What was the date of Kumbhotsavam in 2025?
A: In 2025, Kumbhotsavam was held on April 15 (Tuesday).

Q: What was the date of Kumbhotsavam in 2024?
A: In 2024, Kumbhotsavam was held on April 26 (Friday).

Q: Is Kumbhotsavam always on a Tuesday or Friday?
A: Yes. Temple authorities strictly observe the tradition that Kumbhotsavam must fall on the first Tuesday or Friday after the Chaitra Pournami, whichever arrives first. It is never scheduled on any other day of the week.

--- THE RITUALS & OFFERINGS ---

Q: What is Satvika Bali and why is it used in Kumbhotsavam?
A: Satvika Bali is a non-violent, bloodless form of ritual offering in which vegetarian items (pumpkin, coconuts, lemons, and cooked rice) are offered to the Goddess as a symbolic sacrifice. It replaces the older practice of animal or bird sacrifice, which is now strictly prohibited by law within the Srisailam temple town.

Q: What items are offered as Satvika Bali to Goddess Bhramaramba during Kumbhotsavam?
A: The traditional offerings include pumpkin (gourd), coconuts, lemons, and cooked rice. Additionally, turmeric and kumkum (vermilion) are offered in what is known as "Santhi Prakriya" (peace ritual).

Q: How large is the scale of offerings at Kumbhotsavam?
A: The scale is enormous. In the 2023 Kumbhotsavam, approximately 4,000 pumpkins, more than 2,000 coconuts, nearly one lakh (100,000) lemons, and cooked rice were offered to Goddess Bhramaramba. Local merchant organisations and devotees donate the lemons and pumpkins.

Q: What special poojas are performed during Kumbhotsavam?
A: Temple priests perform Navavarana Archana, Trishati (300-name chanting), Khadgamala (recitation of the goddess's thousand names with each representing a sword), and Ashtottara Shatanama Kumkumarchana (kumkum offering with 108 names) in honour of Goddess Bhramaramba Devi.

Q: What is the Rajaka Rangavalli and when is it performed?
A: Rajaka Rangavalli is a ritualistic decorative rangoli (floor pattern) created by the temple washerman (Rajaka) as part of the Kumbhotsavam tradition. This is a hereditary ritual role performed as part of the festival's prescribed customs.

Q: What happens at the Simha Mandapam during Kumbhotsavam?
A: In the evening segment of Kumbhotsavam, rice is poured as a "Rashi" (heap/mound) at the Simha Mandapam — a traditional ritual act considered auspicious and significant to the festival.

Q: What is the Kumbhaharati procession during Kumbhotsavam?
A: The Kumbhaharati is a unique procession that forms part of the Kumbhotsavam celebrations, in which a man dressed as a woman presents a special offering to Goddess Bhramaramba Devi. This tradition is one of the distinctive and unusual customs of this festival.

Q: What poojas are performed at the Sri Chakram during Kumbhotsavam?
A: Special poojas are conducted at the Sri Chakram (a sacred geometric yantra associated with Goddess worship) as part of the Kumbhotsavam rituals.

Q: What happens to darshan on the day of Kumbhotsavam?
A: On the day of Kumbhotsavam, all special poojas for Goddess Bhramaramba Devi are performed in Ekantam (solitude — without the presence of devotees). Darshan for the general public at the Bhramaramba temple is restricted to only between 7 PM and 9 PM. All arjitha sevas (paid private rituals) at the goddess temple are suspended on that day.

Q: Are services at the Lord Mallikarjuna shrine affected on Kumbhotsavam day?
A: Yes. Arjitha sevas at the Lord Mallikarjuna Swamy temple are also held only until noon on the day of Kumbhotsavam. Darshan and arjitha sevas at the Lord's temple are suspended from the evening. Kalyanotsavam (celestial wedding) and Ekanta Seva of both Lord Mallikarjuna and Goddess Bhramaramba are cancelled for the day.

Q: When do normal temple services resume after Kumbhotsavam?
A: Normal darshan and arjitha sevas at both the Mallikarjuna and Bhramaramba temples resume from 7:00 AM (or 7:30 AM depending on the year) on the morning following Kumbhotsavam. For the 2026 event (April 7), services were set to resume from 7:30 AM on April 8.

--- PRE-FESTIVAL PREPARATIONS ---

Q: Are there any rituals that begin before Kumbhotsavam as a lead-up?
A: Yes. Following the conclusion of the annual Maha Shivaratri Brahmotsavam, coconuts are offered to Goddess Bhramaramba Devi every Tuesday and Friday — this ongoing coconut offering is the traditional preparatory practice that continues until the main Kumbhotsavam day.

Q: Who chairs the preparatory meeting for Kumbhotsavam arrangements?
A: The Temple Executive Officer (EO) chairs the preparatory review meeting. In 2026, the meeting was chaired by Temple Executive Officer M Srinivasa Rao.

--- RULES, RESTRICTIONS & ADMINISTRATION ---

Q: Is animal sacrifice allowed during Kumbhotsavam?
A: No. Animal and bird sacrifice, and any form of violence, are strictly prohibited within the Srisailam temple limits at all times under the Endowments Act. During Kumbhotsavam, enforcement is specially intensified — police, revenue departments, and temple authorities deploy special inspection teams and mobile squads, and display boards are put up across the temple town.

Q: Are transport services monitored during Kumbhotsavam?
A: Yes. Transport (RTC bus) authorities are specifically instructed to prevent carrying animals or birds in their buses arriving at Srisailam during the festival period. Checks are also conducted at tollgates.

Q: Are liquor sales restricted during Kumbhotsavam?
A: Yes. Temple authorities request the District Collector to suspend liquor sales in the Srisailam area on the eve of and on the day of Kumbhotsavam.

Q: What crowd management arrangements are made for Kumbhotsavam?
A: The engineering department sets up additional queue lines along the temple steps. Security personnel implement safety measures. Special focus is given to managing large influxes of devotees particularly during evening darshan hours, which are the only public darshan slots on the festival day.

--- SIGNIFICANCE & CALENDAR CONTEXT ---

Q: Is Kumbhotsavam an annual or one-time event?
A: Kumbhotsavam is an annual festival, observed every year in the month of Chaitra (March–April). It is a recurring, well-established tradition at Srisailam confirmed across multiple consecutive years (2023, 2024, 2025, 2026).

Q: What makes Kumbhotsavam spiritually significant?
A: Kumbhotsavam is performed "for the wellbeing of humankind" — the stated purpose in official temple announcements. The offering of Satvika Bali, the elaborate poojas at the Sri Chakram and Simha Mandapam, and the traditions like Rajaka Rangavalli and Kumbhaharati reflect the festival's deep ritualistic and community significance. It is one of the principal annual festivals dedicated to Goddess Bhramaramba Devi.

Q: How does Kumbhotsavam fit in the Srisailam annual festival calendar?
A: Kumbhotsavam falls after Ugadi (Telugu New Year, Chaitra Shukla Pratipada) and after the Chaitra Pournami (Full Moon of Chaitra), typically in the second half of April. It comes after the Maha Shivaratri Brahmotsavam season and is one of the key Chaitra month observances at Srisailam.

Q: Should devotees planning a visit on Kumbhotsavam day expect full darshan?
A: No. Devotees should be aware that on Kumbhotsavam day, both temples observe reduced and restricted services. Public darshan at the Bhramaramba temple is permitted only between 7 PM and 9 PM. Arjitha (paid) sevas are suspended. Normal services resume from the following morning. Planning a visit on the day after Kumbhotsavam may be preferable for those seeking full darshan and seva access.
